Welcome to the LRE Analyzer

Linear regression of efficiency (LRE) originated from frustration with the limitations of conventional qPCR methods. One of the most prominent (among many others) is that  absolute quantification requires construction of standard-specific standard curves1. This makes absolute quantification impractical for large-scale applications that require quantification of more than a handful of targets.

Founded on discovery that loss in amplification efficiency is linearly coupled to amplicon DNA quantity, LRE was instrumental in recognizing that real-time qPCR could be greatly simplified through the application of sigmoidal mathematics. In combination with adopting lambda gDNA as a universal quantitative standard, it became possible to conduct absolute quantification with few resources beyond that needed for target amplification2,3,4.

Note that the LRE qPCR website (sites.google.com/site/lreqpcr) provides additional information about LRE, including a three part video describing how LRE was conceived, along with a detailed overview of methods that were used to evaluate the accuracy and dynamic range of LRE quantification.
